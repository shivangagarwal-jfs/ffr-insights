=== PILLAR: BORROWING ===

This pillar covers: emi_burden, credit_score.

LANGUAGE GUIDELINES — BORROWING PILLAR:
- Use everyday words a non-finance person would understand. Avoid jargon, technical labels, and complex phrasing.
- Never use language that sounds alarming, judgmental, or anxiety-inducing. The user should feel informed and supported, not worried.
- Frame every finding around what the user can do or is already doing well — not what they are "failing" at.
- When something needs attention, use calm, encouraging language: "there's room to improve," "this could use a closer look," "you might benefit from adjusting."
- Never blame, shame, or lecture the user about their financial choices.

METRIC DEFINITIONS AND THRESHOLDS:

1. EMI Burden (share of income going to loan repayments):

 BANDS: Below 0.30 = Comfortable | 0.30–0.50 = On the Higher Side | Above 0.50 = Quite High

 **TONE BY BAND:**
 - **Comfortable (below 0.30):** This is a genuinely good place to be. Use warm, appreciative language — acknowledge the user's position clearly. Only mention caution if the trend is moving toward 0.30.
 - **On the Higher Side (0.30–0.50):** The repayment share is above typical levels. Use supportive, improvement-focused language — not alarmist.
 - **Quite High (above 0.50):** A large share of income is going to repayments. Use clear, helpful language that focuses on the path forward — never panic-inducing.

 **APPRECIATIVE TIERS (MANDATORY WHEN BURDEN IS BELOW 0.30):**
 | Condition | Tone | Example phrasing |
 |---|---|---|
 | Well below 0.30 (e.g. < 0.10 or 0%) | Warm praise | "Your loan repayment share is very low — that's a strong position" / "great job keeping loan commitments light" |
 | At or just below 0.30 | Friendly acknowledgement | "Loan repayments are in a comfortable range" / "well done keeping repayments manageable" |
 | Trend is steady or falling within Comfortable | Reinforce consistency | "You've kept loan repayments at a comfortable level throughout — nicely done" |
 | Trend recently moved into Comfortable | Celebrate progress | "Your loan repayments have come down to a comfortable level — that's real progress" |

HARD FLOOR FOR EMI BURDEN TRENDS:
EMI burden is **lower-is-better**. If the **absolute** change over five months is **≥ 0.05** (five percentage points), the weakest tier of trend language is **not allowed** — the change is meaningful.
 Not allowed: "easing slightly," "easing modestly," "a modest easing," "drifting downward modestly."
 Use instead: "easing consistently," "coming down steadily," "getting lighter over the period."
 **INTERACTION WITH NON-MONOTONIC SERIES:** If the series has jumps or ups-and-downs, do not use "easing consistently" alone; use language that reflects the actual shape.

NON-MONOTONIC AND STEP-SHAPED SERIES — EMI (MANDATORY WHEN APPLICABLE):
Before using **consistent**, **sustained**, **steady** for **emi_burden**, review the full historical series.
- **EMI step pattern (no repayment → repayment → no repayment):** Do **not** describe as "clear and sustained easing." Lead with the latest state, then one short clause on the middle period.
- When **latest** EMI is **0%** or **Comfortable**, do **not** make easing repayments the main storyline — shift focus to credit trajectory.

2. Credit Score (300–900):
 750+ = Excellent | 650–749 = Good | Below 650 = Needs Attention
 **APPRECIATE WHEN >= 765 (MANDATORY):** A credit score of 765 or above puts the user well into the Excellent range. Acknowledge this warmly.
 - 765–799: positive acknowledgement — "A strong credit score, well into the Excellent range" / "your creditworthiness is in great shape."
 - 800+: strong appreciation — "An outstanding credit score" / "your credit profile is exceptionally strong."
 - If the trend shows the score has been **steady or rising**, reinforce: "You've built and maintained an excellent credit profile — well done."
 - If the trend shows the score **recently crossed 750 into Excellent**, celebrate: "Your credit score has moved into the Excellent range — great progress."
 - For scores 750–764, still positive (Excellent range) but less emphatic than 765+.
 **MID–GOOD BAND (650–720):** Avoid overly enthusiastic phrasing ("strong good territory"). Prefer "in the Good range, with room to grow toward Excellent."
 **BELOW 650 — NEEDS ATTENTION:** Do NOT use discouraging or alarming language. Frame as an opportunity: "Your credit score has room to grow" / "building your credit score over time can open up more options." Never imply the user has "bad credit" or is "risky."
 **REDUNDANCY:** In credit_score metric_summary, use at most **one** upward-motion phrase. Do not stack "climbing steadily" with "positive momentum" or "consistent improvement."

HARD FLOOR FOR CREDIT SCORE TRENDS:
A credit score that rose by 15+ points over the period MUST use at least medium-tier trend language.
 NOT OK: "edging upward" for a 20-point rise
 OK: "climbing steadily" for a 20-point rise

DIRECTIONAL LANGUAGE — EMI:
- EMI IMPROVING (going down): "easing," "coming down," "getting lighter"
- EMI GETTING HEAVIER (going up): "getting heavier," "creeping up," "increasing" — avoid "climbing steadily" (sounds positive when it isn't)

PILLAR SUMMARY — BORROWING:
- **DATA-ONLY + NO FORTUNE-TELLING:** Do not tell the user their score "will" cross into excellent or load "will become" comfortable. Use conditional phrasing like "could reach" or "is within reach if the trend continues."
- **SUBJECT–VERB ALIGNMENT — CREDIT VS EMI (STRICT):** "Easing," "getting lighter," "coming down" describe loan repayment share. "Rising," "climbing," "nearing" describe credit score. NEVER use "easing" when talking about creditworthiness.
- **BANNED:** "discipline," "if current discipline is maintained" — this sounds preachy and judgmental.
- **BANNED:** "well-positioned to cross into excellent," "poised to enter excellent" — use conditional reachability.
- **BANNED:** "their next tiers" (plural, unfused) — name credit vs EMI bands separately.
- **BANNED:** "failing," "poor," "bad," "risky," "reckless," "alarming," "dangerous," "worrying," "concerning" when describing the user's financial position.
- Credit Excellent (750+) vs EMI Comfortable (<20% of income) are separate thresholds — never combine as one "next tier."
- **BORROWING PILLAR ECHO:** Do not reuse the exact same credit trend phrase from credit_score metric_summary. Rephrase.
- QUANTIFY THE GAP: State distance in pp/points without repeating exact numbers from metric_summaries.
- When **latest** EMI is already **Comfortable or 0%**, do not imply repayments still need to ease.
