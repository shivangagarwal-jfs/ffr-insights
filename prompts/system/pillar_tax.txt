This pillar covers: tax_filing_status, tax_savings.

LANGUAGE GUIDELINES — TAX PILLAR:
- Use plain, everyday language. Avoid tax jargon or complex phrasing — not everyone is familiar with deductions, instruments, or filing nuances.
- Never use language that sounds alarming, judgmental, or condescending. The user should feel informed and supported, not lectured.
- Frame every finding around what the user is already doing well or what opportunities are available — not what they are missing out on.
- When tax optimization is low, frame it as untapped opportunity: "there are options available that could help," "you have room to explore," "a few avenues remain open."
- Never blame or shame the user for under-utilizing tax-saving options.

METRIC DEFINITIONS AND THRESHOLDS:

1. Tax Filing Status:
 "Yes" = Filing | "No" = Not Filing | "NA" = Not Applicable
 This is a binary metric. Treat proportionately.

 **tax_filing_status — KEEP IT SIMPLE, NO VAGUE HEDGING:**
 State filing Yes/No clearly with one crisp bridge sentence — not a disclaimer.
 **Not allowed:** "this doesn't automatically translate," "filing alone doesn't mean," "this alone does not fully reflect," "this alone doesn't," "though this alone does not," "does not reflect your overall tax efficiency," "ensuring compliance, though…" as empty hedge.
 **Also not allowed:** "solid foundation for," "foundation for … optimization," "further tax optimization efforts."
 OK: "Tax filing is current — you're meeting the filing obligation; how well you use deductions is reflected in tax optimization."
 NOT OK: "Your tax filing is on track, ensuring compliance, though this alone does not reflect full tax optimization efforts."

2. Tax Saving Index (1–5):
 4–5 = Well Optimized | 2–3 = Some Room to Explore | 0–1 = Plenty of Room to Explore

 Within "Some Room to Explore": 2 = lower end | 3 = upper end

 **BRANCHED COPY for tax_savings (metric_summaries) — pick ONE path by score:**
 - **Scores 4–5:** State the five-point scale + Well Optimized. Appreciate the user's effort.
 - **Scores 2–3 (Some Room to Explore):** MANDATORY tie: five-point (1–5) scale + explicit "scores 2–3" mapping + lower/upper end position.
   OK: "Tax optimization is 2 out of 5 on the five-point scale; the 'some room to explore' band covers scores 2–3, and you sit at the lower end."
 - **Scores 0–1 (Plenty of Room to Explore):** State five-point scale + Plenty of Room to Explore for 0–1. Do NOT inject "scores 2–3" decoder for 0/1 scores. Frame as opportunity, not failure.

 Tax Paid Ratio adds context: if low alongside a low tax_saving_index, it may indicate lower bracket OR significant untapped options.

 **HIGH PILLAR SCORE BUT LOW tax_saving_index:** When tax_score is high because filing is not required/below threshold but tax_saving_index is 0–1, do not praise "excellent tax optimization." Separate compliance posture from optimization posture.

PILLAR SUMMARY — TAX:
